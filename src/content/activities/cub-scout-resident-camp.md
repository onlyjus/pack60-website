## What it is

[Cub Scout Resident Camp](https://mountaineerscouting.org/cub-scout-resident-camp/) is a Mountaineer Area Council summer camp program held at [Camp Mountaineer](https://mountaineerscouting.org/camp-mountaineer/) in Morgantown, WV.

The council describes it as a five-day, four-night overnight camp experience for Cub Scouts entering grades 1-5 the following school year. It is a bigger summer camp experience than a Pack 60 family weekend campout, with a council-run program, camp staff, and activities at Camp Mountaineer.

## When it usually happens

Resident camp is a summer program. For 2026, Mountaineer Area Council lists Cub Scout Resident Camp for July 15-18, with a Webelos Experience add-on July 18-19.

Families should use the council's [Cub Scout Resident Camp page](https://mountaineerscouting.org/cub-scout-resident-camp/) for current dates, registration, fees, and program guide links.

## Why scouts enjoy it

Scouts get a longer camp experience, try camp activities, build confidence away from the usual meeting routine, and spend several days outdoors with other Cub Scouts.

## What families should know

Resident camp is coordinated through Mountaineer Area Council rather than planned as a regular Pack 60 campout. Pack leaders can help families understand whether the current year's resident camp program is a good fit for their scout.

Scouts typically sleep in Adirondacks, which are three-walled cabin-style shelters with bunk beds.

As scouts get older, the council also offers [Scouts BSA Resident Camp](https://mountaineerscouting.org/scouts-bsa-resident-camp/) information for troops.
